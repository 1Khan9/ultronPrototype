# Fracture — Valorant Map Reference

**Codename / in-game ID:** `Jam`  
**Theme:** New Mexico research facility (OMEGA Earth particle accelerator complex split by a dimensional rift)  
**Release:** September 9 2021  
**Pool status (2026):** Inactive in ranked; available in custom/unrated  
**Layout:** H-shaped. Attackers spawn on *both* outer wings (top-left and bottom-right); defenders spawn in the *centre*. Two one-way underground ziplines cross the map under the defender spawn so attackers can swap wings without crossing defender territory.  
**Sites:** A (west/left half), B (east/right half)  
**Ultimate Orbs:** 4 — one in A Hall, one in A Dish, one in B Main (tunnel), one near B Arcade  

---

## Map Layout Overview

```
[ATK Spawn A-Side] ──── A Hall / A Drop ──── A SITE ──── A Link ──── [DEF Spawn] ──── B Link / B Canteen ──── B SITE ──── B Tree ──── [ATK Spawn B-Side]
                              |                                                                                          |
                         (zipline underground — one-way each direction, connecting both ATK spawns)
```

- Attackers can take the **A-side spawn** route (A Hall → A Door → A Rope → A Site, or A Drop → A Site) OR ride the zipline to flip to the **B-side**.
- Defenders rotate through **Defender Spawn** (centre), which is the fastest path between sites.

## A Site — Full Callout List

### A Site (the bombsite itself)
The reactor / spike-plant zone. Contains two plant positions: **Top Site** (upper/heaven-level platform) and **Bottom Site** (lower ground area with pillar cover). Access via A Drop (attackers from above), A Link (defenders), and A Main / A Hall (attackers from the front).

### A Hall
Primary attacker entry corridor leading from the A-side attacker spawn toward the site. Long sightline. Contains one **Ultimate Orb**. Splits into A Door (right/inner) and continues toward A Main (straight).

### A Main
The direct, wide attacker-facing entrance into A Site from A Hall. Longer range engagement, exposed, defenders hold from site or A Rope.

### A Door
Opening in A Hall that connects to A Rope and A Sand; gives attackers a flanking line around the main corridor. A key gateway — defenders try to hold it or smoke it to deny access to Sand.

### A Rope
The rope/climbing position bridging A Door to A Link (and toward Defender Spawn). An elevated angle; used by attackers to gain upper-level control and by defenders as an aggressive peek or rotation to A. Sometimes called **A Short** (the route through A Door → A Rope is the "short" path).

### A Link
The connector passage between A Site and Defender Spawn. Tight, important choke for defenders rotating back and attackers flanking. Smoked frequently.

### A Drop
The elevated ledge/drop-down entry onto A Site coming from the top (attacker side) via the B-side zipline exit or from above the site. Attackers drop down; defenders can pre-aim it. Accessible when attackers ride the zipline from B-side spawn and exit on the A elevation.

### A Dish
Side area of A Site — lower/flank flank route, contains a second **Ultimate Orb**. "Least contested" but important for map control; attackers coming through A Dish can force a crossfire with A Hall pushes. Near **A Gate**.

### A Gate
The opening/angle near A Dish, close to the CT/Defender Spawn side. Early peeks happen here; defenders can catch rotating or flanking attackers.

### Sand
The area accessed through A Door, between A Door and the site proper. A control point that gives attackers a foothold before committing to site. Defenders try to deny access to Sand from A Door.

### A Heaven / Top Site
The elevated platform on A Site. Provides overwatch on A Main, A Drop, A Link, and most of the bombsite. Spike can be planted here. (Also called **Heaven** or **Top**.)

### A Hell / Bottom Site
The lower ground level of A Site. Harder sightlines from heaven; spike can be planted here. (Also called **Hell** or **Bottom**.)

### Stairs (A Site)
Vertical connector on A Site linking Bottom Site (hell) to Top Site (heaven). Position contesters fight for staircase control on site.

### A Cubby
Small recessed corner/hiding spots on A Site used by defenders for post-plant or lurk setups.

## B Site — Full Callout List

### B Site (the bombsite itself)
The spike-plant zone on the B (east) half of the map. Accessed via B Arcade (left/top lane), B Main (centre/direct), and B Tunnel (underground from below). Defenders hold from B Tower, B Generator, B Canteen, and B Link.

### B Main
The primary attacker entry corridor to B Site, approaching from the B-side attacker spawn (bottom-right spawn octagon). Contains one **Ultimate Orb** in the tunnel portion. Wide-ish approach, commonly smoked to push through.

### B Tunnel / Underpass
The underground passage that runs beneath B Site, connecting the bottom of B Main to B Arcade. Attackers use it to flank or split from B Main; defenders use it for safe rotation. Sometimes called **Underpass**. Parallels the B spike site overhead.

### B Arcade
One of the most important areas on B. It is the upper/left approach corridor to B Site from the B-side attacker spawn (top wing). Presents three sub-lanes: **Underpass** (left, leads to B Main), **centre lane** (straight into site), and a right turn **up into B Tower**. Contains one **Ultimate Orb** nearby.

### B Tower
Elevated vantage structure on B Site, accessible from B Arcade (right side). Critical control point — you cannot safely plant or defuse without clearing or covering Tower. Gives overwatch on B Bench, the plant zone, and B Generator. Defenders hold here; attackers contest it to take site.

### B Generator
A defensive holding position near B Canteen, further from the site toward Defender Spawn. Angles into B Link and parts of the site. Attackers must clear or smoke it when pushing. Used for retakes.

### B Canteen
The outer entry area / room connecting B Link to the site approach. A popular defensive spot — rotating defenders can hide in Canteen and hold B Long / B Main angles. Common smoke placement to cut off this angle.

### B Link
The connector passage between B Site and Defender Spawn (mirroring A Link on the A side). Defenders rotate through B Link. Tight choke; smoked during site pushes.

### B Tree
The top/upper area connecting to B Main from the B-side attacker spawn (top octagon). From B Tree, attackers can run down B Main (straight) or enter B Tunnel to flank through the underpass. Prominent tree feature provides some cover.

### B Bench
A defender hiding position at B Site, on the attacker-approach side. From Bench, a defender can watch Arcade, Underpass, and Tower approaches. "Hiding spot" — used for post-plant or lurk. Attacker-spawn-side vantage point.

### Double Box
Cover position near the B Long / B Main entrance. Two stacked boxes providing cover for an attacker peeking into B Site from B Main. Used for mid-push cover.

## Attacker Spawn Areas

### Attacker Spawn A-Side (Top Octagon)
The attacker starting position on the *top/left* wing of the H. Routes out: forward to **A Hall** (→ A Main, A Drop) and downward to **B Tree** (→ B Main). Also houses the **A-side zipline** entry.

### Attacker Spawn B-Side (Bottom Octagon)
The attacker starting position on the *bottom/right* wing of the H. Routes out: upward to **B Arcade** (→ B Tower, B Main) and forward to **B Main** directly. Also houses the **B-side zipline** entry.

### Bridge
The connector/transition area within or near attacker spawn linking the two spawn octagon structures. Sometimes referred to in context of spawn-side movement.

---

## Defender Spawn

### Defender Spawn (CT / Centre)
The central position of the map. Defenders start here every round and have the shortest rotation path to either site. Connects to **A Link** (toward A Site) and **B Link** (toward B Site). Also called **CT Spawn** or **Mid**.

---

## Ziplines

### Ziplines (Underground / Cross-Map)
Two one-directional ziplines run underground across the full width of the map, beneath Defender Spawn, connecting one attacker octagon to the other. Rules:
- **One-way only** — you cannot stop or reverse mid-line.
- **Cannot jump off** mid-travel.
- Travel is fast — near-instant cross-map rotation for attackers.
- Entry/exit on each end; defenders cannot use them.
- Allows attackers to fake a site, ride the zipline, and attack the opposite site from the unexpected direction.

| Term | Meaning on Fracture |
|------|-------------------|
| **Heaven** | Elevated area on a site (A Top Site / A Heaven) |
| **Hell** | Ground-level area on a site below Heaven (A Bottom Site / A Hell) |
| **CT** | Defender Spawn / defender-side rotations |
| **Mid** | Defender Spawn centre area (Fracture's centre IS the defender spawn) |
| **Orb** | Ultimate Orb — 4 total (A Hall, A Dish, B Main tunnel, B Arcade area) |
| **Zipline** / **Zip** | Underground attacker cross-map transit |
| **Top side** | A-side (left half of the H) |
| **Bottom side** | B-side (right half of the H) |
| **Retake** | Defenders re-entering a lost site; key spots are B Generator, B Canteen, A Link |
| **Lurk** | Flanking from an unexpected position; Canteen, Bench, Sand are popular lurk spots |
| **Pincer / Split** | Attacking a site from two directions simultaneously (Fracture's core mechanic) |

## Agent Meta on Fracture (2025-2026 context)

Fracture's dual-spawn flanking pressure and tight corridors favour:

**Controllers (smokes):** Brimstone, Astra, Viper — key chokes: A Link, A Door, B Canteen, B Generator, B Arcade entry  
**Sentinels:** Cypher (A Link, B Link trips); Killjoy (site lockdown B Tower/Generator); Chamber (B Tower Op/Sheriff)  
**Initiators:** Sova (A Hall recon bolts; B Main orb lineup), Breach (stun A Hall/B Arcade), KAY/O (suppress defender angles), Fade  
**Duelists:** Jett (B Tower Op play), Raze (A Drop/B Arcade blasts), Reyna (lurk paths), Neon (zipline rotation synergy)

**Key notes:**
- 4 ultimate orbs makes Sova's Recon Bolt and Owl Drone extremely valuable for locating defenders.
- B Tower is Fracture's single most contested piece of ground; whoever holds it dictates B site.
- Ziplines enable a unique fake-execute: commit one side, zip across, hit the other site before defenders can rotate.
