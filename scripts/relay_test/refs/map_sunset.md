# Valorant — Sunset Map Reference

**Status:** Current as of June 2026  
**Pool status:** Out of active competitive rotation as of Patch 12.00 (January 2026) — fully playable in Unrated, Swiftplay, Spike Rush, Escalation  
**Defining feature:** Mechanical destructible door between B Market and Mid Courtyard (500 HP; permanently open once destroyed)  
**Setting:** Los Angeles, California — Boyle Heights neighbourhood; Art Deco + Mission Revival architecture; Kingdom Corporation West Facility  
**Theme:** Neon sunsets, LA street art murals (OG-Slick, _cryptik_, ATLAS, 123KLAN), food trucks, boba shops, skate shop, traffic, graffiti

---

## Map Overview

Sunset is a traditional two-site, three-lane map with no gimmicks (no teleporters, zip lines, or rotating doors). It is the tenth standard map released in VALORANT, launching in Patch 7.04 (August 29, 2023) during Episode 7 Act 2. The layout is similar in spirit to Ascent: both sites connect directly to attacker and defender spawns, with mid acting as a second-path connector to both sites.

**Bomb sites:** A (left/west) and B (right/east)  
**Site count:** 2  
**Mid structure:** Horseshoe-shaped courtyard with attacker and defender access points at both ends

**Layout sketch (top = attacker side, bottom = defender side):**

```
[Attacker Spawn]
       |
   A Lobby ──── A Main ──── A Elbow ──── A Site ──── A Alley ────┐
       |                                                           |
   Mid Tiles ──── Mid Courtyard ──── [Market Door] ──── B Market ─┤
       |                |                                          |
   Mid Bottom       Mid Top ──────────────────────── B Boba ──────┤
                        |                                          |
                [Defender Spawn] ─────────────── B Main ──── B Site
```

Mid control determines round pace. Losing mid early opens both attacker split routes and uncomfortable defender rotations. The Market Door on the B Market / Mid Courtyard boundary is the map's single interactive mechanical element.

## A Site Area

A Site is the smaller of the two bomb sites. It features a relatively open layout and longer sightlines than B, favouring rifle duels. A Main is a tight lane that funnels attackers, while A Elbow and A Link provide secondary access. Defenders often hold from CT/Defender Spawn rotation through A Alley, or contest from A Elbow.

### A Site Named Locations

| Callout | Also Called | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **A Lobby** | A Att Lobby | Attacker-side entry corridor before A Main; connects attacker spawn to A Main and also links to Mid Tiles |
| **A Main** | A Hall | Primary attacker lane into A Site; a straight corridor that creates tight 50/50 angles on entry; most early-round A-side action occurs here |
| **A Elbow** | Elbow | Corner/angle position connecting A Main to A Alley behind A Site; critical choke — attackers should secure it as fast as possible; defenders can play early aggression here |
| **A Link** | A High, A Upper | Secondary route from mid (Mid Tiles side) into A Site; elevated passage above A Main level; allows top-side access to site and crossfire support |
| **A Alley** | CT Alley, Alley | Narrow passage behind A Site connecting to Defender Spawn; gives defenders an angle into attackers from the rear; also used by defenders rotating from spawn onto site |
| **A Site** | A, Bomb Site A | The spike plant area; smallest bombsite on the map; contains a generator/equipment prop in the centre that players use as reference ("generator") and vertical boxes; open enough for rifle engagements |

| **A Green Boxes** | Green Boxes | Stack of green/military crates on A Site; provide cover and allow elevated angles; key cover for both attackers planting and defenders retaking |
| **A Windows** | Windows | Small window openings on the wall between A Main and A Link; used for utility (peeks, flashes, dart lineups) |

## B Site Area

B Site is the larger and more complex of the two sites. It revolves around a giant clock tower/pillar in the centre. The site has multiple corners and is congested — attackers must clear numerous angles on entry. B Boba and B Market both connect B Site to the mid and defender-spawn area, giving defenders rapid access.

| **B Main** | B Hall | Primary attacker lane into B Site; split into two corridors/rooms at entry, providing slightly staggered paths onto site; critical control point for attackers |
| **B Site** | B, Bomb Site B | The spike plant area; larger than A with more corners; features the central clock tower/pillar and restricted plant zones |

## Mid Area

Mid on Sunset is horseshoe-shaped and connects A Lobby (via Mid Tiles) on one side to Defender Spawn (via Mid Top) and B Boba on the other. It is not inherently defender- or attacker-favoured — it suits rifle ranges. The team that controls Mid Courtyard can split-execute onto either site and force defenders into awkward rotations. The Market Door is the defining interactive feature in mid.

### Mid Named Locations

| Callout | Also Called | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Mid Courtyard** | Mid, Courtyard | The central open area of mid; connects Mid Tiles (attacker side), Mid Top (defender side), and B Market (via the Market Door); heart of the map — losing it concedes multiple approach angles |
| **Mid Tiles** | Tiles | Attacker-side entry into mid; tiled flooring section; connects from A Lobby side; provides approach path toward Mid Courtyard and a secondary A Link angle |
| **Mid Bottom** | Mid Att, Bottom | Lower/attacker-side entry into mid from B Lobby direction; where attackers initially push into mid from the B side of attacker spawn |
| **Mid Top** | Top Mid, Top | Defender-side opening into mid; connects Defender Spawn to Mid Courtyard; gives defenders an aggressive early presence in mid or a passive hold position; crossfire with Mid Tiles/Bottom |
| **Mid Pillar** | Pillar | A structural pillar or cover element within Mid Courtyard; used as reference for positioning and as cover during mid fights |
| **Market** | B Market, Market Area | The indoor section adjacent to B Site with the mechanical door facing Mid Courtyard; also sometimes called "Market" without a site prefix; not to be confused with B Site itself |
| **Market Door** | The Door, B Door, Mid Door | The mechanical destructible door between B Market and Mid Courtyard; **500 HP**; can be opened/closed via a control panel/switch on the defender's side inside Market; once destroyed it is permanently open for the rest of the round; opens/closing creates sound cues; strategically pivotal — closed door restricts mid-to-B paths, open/destroyed door creates a direct mid-to-B Market sightline |
| **Door Control** | Switch, Control Panel | The interactable switch panel on the defender's side inside B Market that controls the Market Door open/close state |

```
Attacker Spawn
├── → A Lobby → A Main → A Elbow → A Site
│                           └──── A Link (upper) → A Site
├── → Mid Tiles → Mid Courtyard
│                     ├── Mid Top ↔ Defender Spawn
│                     └── [Market Door] → B Market → B Site
└── → B Lobby → Mid Bottom → Mid Courtyard
         └──── B Main → B Site

Defender Spawn
├── → A Alley → A Site (rear)
├── → Mid Top → Mid Courtyard
└── → B Boba → B Market / B Site
```

Key rotation note: Defenders rotate A → B fastest via Defender Spawn through B Boba. Defender Spawn is the fastest cross-map route.

## Special Mechanics

### Market Door
- **Location:** Between B Market and Mid Courtyard
- **HP:** 500
- **Control:** Switch panel on defender side inside Market (defenders can open/close it; attackers must destroy it)
- **Destruction:** Permanently open once HP reaches zero; cannot be repaired mid-round
- **Sound cue:** Opening/closing the door produces audible noise; skilled players use this for information
- **Strategic impact:**
  - Closed door: Cuts off direct mid-to-B sightline; forces attackers to clear Market entry
  - Open door: Creates a crossfire angle from mid into B Market
  - Destroyed door: Permanently opens the mid-to-B Market path, changing every subsequent round sightline

## Key Sightlines and Angles

### A Site
- **A Main → A Site:** Attackers fight the ~50-degree angle on entry; defenders hold from the default box or generator cover
- **A Elbow peek:** Defenders at A Elbow can prefire A Main; attackers must clear it before entry
- **A Link → A Site upper:** Allows flanking angle above A Main entry
- **A Alley → A Site rear:** Defender rotation angle; attackers posting in Alley catch rotating defenders

### Mid
- **Mid Top → Mid Tiles/Bottom:** Defenders at Mid Top can contest attackers pushing Mid Bottom or Mid Tiles across the courtyard
- **Mid Courtyard → B Market (through door):** Long mid-to-B sightline when door is open/destroyed
- **Mid Tiles → A Link:** Attackers who take Mid Tiles can threaten A Link, opening a second angle on A Site

### B Site
- **B Main → B Site:** Tight entry corridor; multiple staggered angles including B Pillar and B Stairs
- **B Boba → B Market:** Quick defender rotation angle; also used by attackers to split B
- **B Backsite:** Deep positions behind B Pillar; favourite anchor/post-plant spot

## Common Strategies

### Attacking
- **A Execute:** 3 through A Main + 2 through A Link (mid control required); smokes on A Elbow and A Alley CT; mollies site corners
- **B Execute:** 3 B Main + 2 B Market (mid control required); flash into B Main entry, smoke B Boba rotation
- **Mid Split:** Control Mid Courtyard, send 2 to A Link and 3 through Market/B Short; forces defenders to pick a site without full rotation time
- **B Boba Split:** 2 push B Main, 2 push B Boba from mid, 1 stays mid; splits B defenders from multiple angles simultaneously
- **Verbatim relay callouts:** "A lobby," "A main," "A elbow," "A site," "A alley," "mid tiles," "mid courtyard," "mid top," "B lobby," "B main," "B market," "B boba," "B site"

### Defending
- **Standard:** 2 on A (one A Elbow, one rotating from CT/Alley), 1 mid top, 2 on B
- **Market Door strategy:** Defenders close the door early to deny mid-B crossfire; open it to reveal mid; destroy it as attackers approach B to remove their cover
- **Retake A:** Coordinate a push through CT (A Alley) and A Link simultaneously; use molly/KJ lockdown to clear generator and boxes
- **Retake B:** Re-enter from B Boba (spawn side) and B Market (mid side); flash/KJ turret to clear B Backsite; Lockdown for spike-defuse cover

## Lore Context

Sunset depicts the VALORANT Protocol's first direct mission against Kingdom Corporation, invading the Kingdom West Facility in Los Angeles (Boyle Heights) — the primary site for Project Landfall development. The map's street art features works by OG-Slick (mural at the attacker-spawn Daytrip Skate shop), _cryptik_ (spiral script piece at the Mantis Ltd. Vintage shop in attacker spawn), and ATLAS (cat characters near Defender Spawn). Development versions of the map included acid pools that could damage players, later removed.
