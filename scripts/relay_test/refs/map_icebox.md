# Valorant — Icebox Map Reference

**Status:** Current as of June 2026  
**Pool status:** Currently INACTIVE in competitive rotation (removed with Patch 11.04); available in Unrated, Swiftplay, Spike Rush, Escalation  
**Defining feature:** Heavy verticality — horizontal and vertical ziplines, elevated nests on both sites, multi-level planting zones  
**Setting:** Kingdom Corporation arctic research and radianite transport facility, Siberia, Russia (East Siberian Sea island — coordinates 76°44′N 149°30′E)  
**Theme:** Frozen industrial port, shipping containers, cranes, frozen ship hull, samurai artifact display (B site), sub-zero Arctic environment

---

## Map Overview

Icebox is a two-site map with a strong horizontal layout split across a frozen industrial complex. The map is notable for its extensive verticality — both bomb sites feature elevated "nest" positions accessed by ziplines or ropes, and a unique horizontal zipline spans the mid section. It is considered one of the most attacker-sided maps in Valorant.

**Layout sketch (top = attacker side, bottom = defender side):**
```
[Attacker Spawn]
      |              \
  A Main/Belt      B Main (Green/Yellow)
      |                    |
  A Site (Nest/Pipes/Generator/Screens/Heaven)     B Site (Snowman/Orange/Tube/Kitchen)
      |                    |
    Maze ───────── Mid Tube / Boiler / Blue ─────── Kitchen
                          |
                   [Defender Spawn]
```

**Flow summary:**
- Attackers reach **A site** via A Main and the Belt zipline, or up through Nest ropes
- Attackers reach **B site** via B Green / B Yellow lanes, or rotate through Mid Tube + Kitchen
- Mid is the connector hub — Mid Tube links directly to Defender Spawn, B Tube top, and Kitchen
- Defenders spawn closer to B site than attackers; rotating to A is slower

---

## Lore & Theme Details

- **Operator:** Kingdom Corporation (antagonist faction)
- **Purpose:** Radianite storage, research, and shipment; also an archaeological excavation site
- **Notable find:** Ancient Japanese shipwreck (with samurai armour sets embedded with Radianite) frozen into the B site structure — linked to Yoru's lore (he stole his mask from the display case)
- **Visual landmarks:** Shipping containers (yellow, green, orange), frozen cranes, industrial boilers, snow-covered terrain, display chamber (A site), frozen ship hull (B site), radianite crates everywhere
- **Ziplines (unique mechanic):** Two vertical ziplines ascend to A Nest; one horizontal zipline traverses the mid/belt area; ascending/descending both make noise

## Agent Recommendations for Icebox

The map heavily rewards agents with vertical mobility, long-range recon, smoke placement for chokepoints, and utility for clearing the niche elevated positions.

### Tier 1 (Strongest picks)

| Agent | Role | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Viper** | Controller | Toxic Screen covers the length of B main or A main; Poison Cloud blocks single chokepoints (Tube, Kitchen entry); Snake Bite strong post-plant; wall can block zipline approaches |
| **Sage** | Sentinel | Wall blocks belt/zipline and Generator approaches; Slow Orbs shut down Kitchen and Tube pushes; Barrier Orb can create platforms for new angles; strong A and B plant zone control |
| **Sova** | Initiator | Recon Bolt clears Nest, Kitchen, and under-Tube; long sightlines suit Owl Drone and Hunter's Fury; strong on a map where enemies hide in vertical positions |
| **Killjoy** | Sentinel | Nanoswarm traps cover Tube, Kitchen, and post-plant zones; Turret watches multiple angles; Lockdown useful for retakes |

### Tier 2 (Strong situational picks)

| Agent | Role | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Jett** | Duelist | Operator-friendly map; Updraft enables unexpected Belt/Nest angles; Tailwind for repositioning after picks; can sit on top of Tube |
| **Omen** | Controller | Shrouded Step over obstacles and through walls; Dark Cover smokes block Tube and Belt; can teleport to unexpected angles |
| **KAY/O** | Initiator | ZERO/point suppresses Nest and Kitchen; FLASH/drive blinds tight chokepoints; NULL/cmd enables team push through tight spaces |
| **Raze** | Duelist | Paint Shells flush Generator and Cubby; Boom Bot useful in Kitchen and under Tube; Blast Pack mobility on verticals |
| **Reyna** | Duelist | Aggressive entry playstyle suits the narrow corridors; Leer on A Main and B Garage; heal/dismiss for trade-heavy duels |
| **Gekko** | Initiator | Mosh Pit flushes Generator / Cubby / Kitchen; Wingman plants spike; Dizzy blinds in narrow corridors |

### Tier 3 (Viable)

| Agent | Role | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Clove** | Controller | Smokes even from death; useful for B site retakes and crossing tube |
| **Harbor** | Controller | Wall for site rushes; Cove blocks Rafters / Heaven angles |
| **Chamber** | Sentinel | Operator suits the long sightlines (Belt, Mid, B Main); Trademark trap holds Kitchen or Tube; TP escape from risky positions |
| **Cypher** | Sentinel | Tripwires cover Tube and Kitchen effectively; Cage control of post-plant zones; Spycam watching nest or boiler |
| **Breach** | Initiator | Fault Line shakes Nest and Kitchen; Flashpoint through walls; useful for aggressive Kitchen takes |
| **Fade** | Initiator | Haunt clears Nest/Kitchen; Prowlers chase into corridors; Seize reveals elevated positions |
| **Skye** | Initiator | Trailblazer into Nest; Guiding Light flashes Kitchen; Seekers locate defenders on rotations |

### Generally Weaker on Icebox

| Agent | Reason |
|---|---|
| **Phoenix** | Curved walls/structures limit wall and run-it-back effectiveness; narrow site entries don't amplify Hot Hands |
| **Yoru** | Decoy usage limited by tight corridors; fakeouts less convincing on a map where audio cues through tube/nest are critical |
| **Neon** | Speed advantage lessened by tight chokepoints; sightlines not as open as maps where she shines |
| **Brimstone** | Stim Beacon less impactful; orbital strike strong post-plant on A but limited by shorter range vs Viper's flexibility |
| **Astra** | Stars require careful placement; Viper outclasses her on this map specifically |

## Common Strategies

### Attacking A Site
- **Belt rush:** Majority pushes up A Main and immediately takes Belt, using it to elevate and contest Nest; smoke Generator / Heaven
- **Nest take:** Zipline up to Nest, plant inside Nest for a brutal post-plant angle
- **Split A via Mid:** One player gets Boiler control; teammates push A Main while mid player flanks through Maze/Screens
- **Viper wall:** Run Viper wall along A Main to belt, blocking Heaven and Rafters sightlines

### Attacking B Site
- **Green + Yellow split:** Two players each take a lane in B Main; force defenders to pick a side
- **Mid + B split:** Two go Kitchen, three go B Main; Kitchen player drops onto B after Tube control
- **Under Tube rush:** Fast path through Under Tube to pressure Kitchen and B simultaneously; risky (many angles)

### Defending A Site
- **Belt hold:** Sentinel/rifler holds Belt to prevent elevation control
- **Heaven hold:** Operator or rifler on Heaven covers Belt, Pipes, and Nest at once
- **Nest aggressive:** One defender plays in Nest early to gather info and trade

### Defending B Site
- **Kitchen priority:** Getting and HOLDING Kitchen is paramount — it cuts off the mid rotation path
- **Snowman hold:** Long angle catches enemies crossing Green-to-Yellow
- **Cubby aggro:** Very forward play; high-risk, high-reward; two defenders can double-peek and trade
