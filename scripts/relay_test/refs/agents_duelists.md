# Valorant — Duelist Agents Reference

**Status:** Current as of June 2026 (Patch 12.x era)
**Role definition:** Duelists are self-sufficient fraggers designed to win individual fights, create space, and entry-frag. Their kit gives them tools to engage aggressively and survive the fight long enough to secure a kill. They are the primary entry-fraggers; their job is to win the first duel so the rest of the team can follow.

**Current duelist roster (8 agents):** Jett, Phoenix, Raze, Reyna, Yoru, Neon, Iso, Waylay

---

## General Duelist Comm Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Entry / Entry frag | First player to push onto a site; win or die creating info |
| Take space | Advance into uncontrolled territory |
| Space gained | Confirmation that the site / chokepoint is safe to push |
| Trade | Kill the enemy who just killed a teammate; at minimum expected of every entry |
| Duel | A 1v1 engagement — duelists are expected to win these |
| Ult up | Agent's ultimate ability is fully charged and ready |
| Ult down / used | Enemy agent just used their ultimate |
| Save dash / save ult | Do not consume the signature/ult; preserve it for a later key moment |
| On retake | Rotating back to recapture a site that has been taken |
| Contact | Enemy is actively pushing / making contact with your position |
| Clear / clear angles | Eliminate or verify all off-angles before committing further |
| Peeking | Moving out to a sightline to gather info or pick a duel |
| Off-angle | Unexpected position not on the standard expected sightline |
| Swing | An aggressive peek or push |
| Jiggle peek | Quick peek and retreat to bait a shot / gather info |
| Dry peek | Peek with no supporting utility |
| Prefiring | Shooting a corner/angle before the enemy appears |
| Tagged / tagged out | Enemy is low health after taking damage |
| One shot | Enemy is on very low HP — one bullet to kill |
| No armor | Enemy has no shield |

## 1. Jett

**Origin:** South Korea
**Real name:** Han Sunwoo
**Lore:** An elite government assassin who uses wind powers to dominate fights. Self-reliant and aggressive; her kit enables aggressive peeking, aerial plays, and instant escapes.
**Role emphasis:** Operator / aggressive peeker; the definitive hard-entry duelist. Blade Storm enables operator aggression without penalty; Tailwind is the escape after the pick.

### Abilities

| Key | Name | Type | Cost | Charges | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C | Cloudburst | Basic | 200 | 2 | Throw a projectile that expands into a brief vision-blocking cloud on impact. Hold to curve the smoke toward crosshair. Duration ~2.5s. |
| Q | Updraft | Basic | 150 | 1 | Instantly propel Jett high into the air. Good for aerial angles and roof positions. |
| E | Tailwind | Signature | Free | 1 (regained by 2 kills) | Activate to begin a 12s window; re-use to dash in the direction Jett is moving. 0.75s activation delay before dash window opens. |
| X | Blade Storm | Ultimate | 8 pts | — | Equip a set of highly accurate throwing knives. Left-click throws a single knife; alt-fire throws all remaining knives simultaneously. Kills recharge knives. Alternate-fire kills do NOT recharge. |

### Key Mechanics
- **Cloudburst** is Jett's personal smoke; used to cross sightlines solo without needing a controller's help.
- **Updraft** can be double-cast in quick succession (two charges stack) for tall aerial positions.
- **Tailwind** activates on a short delay — Jett must expose herself for 0.75s before the dash fires. Kills recharge the dash.
- **Blade Storm** gives Jett perfect accuracy while moving, making it the definitive Operator-aggression tool. The alt-fire fan throw is a panic close-range burst that can one-shot.

### Common Comm Phrases
- "Jett smokes / popping smoke" → enemy Jett deploying Cloudburst
- "Jett in the air / she went up" → Updraft used
- "Jett dashed / dash used / dash burned" → Tailwind consumed; now she has no escape
- "Jett no dash" → critical intel; she cannot escape peeks
- "She has dash / dash up" → Tailwind recharged
- "Jett ult / blades out / blade storm" → enemy activating Blade Storm
- "She's opping with ult" → using Blade Storm knives as sniper substitute
- "Drop / hold the drop" → watch for Jett jumping from elevated positions
- "Take space while she's smoked" → use Jett's own smoke as cover
- "Jett going aggressive / she's peeking" → expect Tailwind dash escape after peek

## 2. Phoenix

**Origin:** United Kingdom
**Real name:** Jamie Adeyemi
**Lore:** A brash UK street performer and Radiant whose fire powers both damage enemies and heal himself. Cocky, loud, and self-assured.
**Role emphasis:** Self-sustaining duelist; his fire abilities heal him, enabling aggressive plays. Run it Back is a safety net for high-risk entry attempts.

### Abilities

| Key | Name | Type | Cost | Charges | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C | Blaze | Basic | 150 | 1 | Cast a curving line of fire forward that blocks vision and damages players passing through it. Hold to bend wall direction. Also heals Phoenix while he stands in it. |
| Q | Hot Hands | Basic | 200 | 1 | Throw a fireball that explodes after a short delay or on hitting the ground, creating a fire zone. Damages enemies; heals Phoenix. Alt-fire for lob trajectory. |
| E | Curveball | Signature | Free (250 to buy extra) | 2 (recharged by 2 kills) | Throw a curving flare that blinds any player who looks at it. Left-click curves left; alt-fire curves right. 1.5s blind duration. |
| X | Run it Back | Ultimate | 6 pts | — | Place a marker at current location. During the 10s active window: if Phoenix dies or the timer expires, he respawns at the marker with full health and shields from cast moment. Kills extend the timer. |

### Key Mechanics
- **Blaze** is both a vision blocker and a personal heal — Phoenix can stand in his own wall to recover HP mid-fight.
- **Hot Hands** on a friendly spot lets Phoenix regenerate HP while waiting; on an enemy position it zones and damages.
- **Curveball** is a two-charge flash; extremely hard to dodge when curved correctly because it can go around corners. Both charges recharge on kills.
- **Run it Back** is a free respawn — Phoenix can take extremely aggressive, otherwise-suicidal peeks. If he dies, he comes back at full HP from the marker location.

### Common Comm Phrases
- "Phoenix flash / flashing right (or left)" → Curveball incoming; call direction so teammates pre-aim or look away
- "Phoenix blind / flash out" → Curveball in flight
- "He's in his wall / walling" → Blaze deployed; he's healing or creating cover
- "Phoenix molly / fire down" → Hot Hands on the ground
- "He's healing" → Phoenix standing in Blaze or Hot Hands zone
- "Phoenix ult / run it back / he's ulting" → Run it Back activated
- "Kill him before timer" / "Clock him" → take Phoenix out while Run it Back is active so he doesn't respawn
- "He came back / respawn" → Run it Back triggered a respawn
- "Phoenix ult down" → Run it Back timer expired without a respawn; ability wasted
- "One flash left / out of flashes" → ability tracking on Curveball charges

## 3. Raze

**Origin:** Brazil (Salvador, Bahia)
**Real name:** Tayane Alves
**Lore:** An energetic mechanical genius who weaponizes her own explosive inventions. She approaches every fight with reckless joy.
**Role emphasis:** Aggressive fragging and area denial. Blast Pack is both a damage tool and a mobility tool (rocket-jumping). Showstopper is a room-clearing ultimate.

### Abilities

| Key | Name | Type | Cost | Charges | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C | Boom Bot | Basic | 300 | 1 | Deploy a bot that travels forward on the ground, bouncing off walls. Auto-locks onto enemies in its frontal cone and chases them. Detonates on contact for heavy damage. 100 HP, 5s duration. |
| Q | Blast Pack | Basic | 200 | 2 | Throw a sticky explosive that attaches to surfaces or agents. Re-use to detonate — deals damage and applies heavy knockback to anything in range. Can be used for rocket-jump mobility. Max 5s deployment. |
| E | Paint Shells | Signature | Free (2 kills to recharge) | 1 | Throw a cluster grenade that detonates on impact, splitting into 4 sub-munitions that each deal independent damage. Can clear corners and lineups. |
| X | Showstopper | Ultimate | 8 pts | — | Equip a rocket launcher and fire a rocket that deals massive area-of-effect damage on contact. Essentially a one-shot kill in the blast radius. 10s duration. |

### Key Mechanics
- **Boom Bot** auto-seeks; use it to clear corners and gather intel. An enemy shooting the bot reveals their position via audio cue.
- **Blast Pack** can be self-detonated for high-speed rocket jumps. Two charges allow double boosts for crossing large gaps or reaching elevated positions very quickly.
- **Paint Shells** is devastating in tight choke points — the sub-munitions spread unpredictably in enclosed areas.
- **Showstopper** is a hard-commit ability; Raze must aim carefully. Blast Pack can be used to reposition after firing.

### Common Comm Phrases
- "Raze bot / Boom Bot out" → bot deployed; location info incoming
- "Bot's chasing / bot locked" → Boom Bot has locked onto an enemy
- "Blast pack / she's packing" → Blast Pack thrown
- "Raze boost / she jumped" → Blast Pack used for mobility boost (rocket-jump)
- "Paint shells / nade" → Paint Shells grenade thrown
- "Cluster incoming / cluster hit" → Paint Shells detonated
- "Raze ult / showstopper / rocket" → Showstopper activated
- "Rocket out / dodge the rocket" → Showstopper missile in flight
- "Raze ult down / rocket used" → Showstopper fired or expired
- "She's boosting through" → Raze using Blast Pack to fly into site

## 4. Reyna

**Origin:** Mexico
**Real name:** Zyanya Mondragón
**Lore:** A powerful Radiant who feeds on enemy life force. Cold, voracious, and ruthless. Her abilities depend entirely on securing kills — she is a snowball agent.
**Role emphasis:** Hard snowball duelist. Incredibly strong when winning; nearly useless when losing. The premier solo-carry agent for ranked; team-reliant comps often run different picks.

### Soul Orb Mechanic
Soul Orbs drop when Reyna eliminates an enemy or damages an enemy who dies within 3 seconds. Orbs last 3 seconds and must be consumed quickly. They fuel both Devour and Dismiss. During Empress, orbs are consumed automatically and infinitely.

### Abilities

| Key | Name | Type | Cost | Charges | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C | Leer | Basic | 250 | 2 | Equip and throw an ethereal, destructible eyeball forward through surfaces. The eye applies Nearsight (severe vision reduction) to all enemies who look at it. Duration 2s. Enemies can destroy the eye by shooting it. |
| Q | Devour | Basic | 200 | 2 | Consume a nearby Soul Orb. Rapidly heals Reyna up to 100 HP (can overheal to 150 with temporary bonus armor). During Empress, auto-heals without consuming orb. |
| E | Dismiss | Signature | 200 (free charges via kills) | 2 | Consume a nearby Soul Orb. Become intangible (invulnerable to damage) for 1.5s. During Empress, also grants full invisibility. |
| X | Empress | Ultimate | 6 pts | — | Enter a frenzy: +25% fire rate, equip speed, and reload speed. Duration indefinite — renews upon killing an enemy or damaging an enemy within 3s of their death. Grants infinite Devour/Dismiss charges. |

### Key Mechanics
- **Leer** fires through walls, allowing remote nearsighting of enemies holding angles. Enemies who don't look away are temporarily blinded; but if they shoot the eye immediately, the effect ends early.
- **Devour** and **Dismiss** require a Soul Orb — Reyna MUST get kills to use her defensive abilities.
- **Dismiss** makes Reyna intangible and lets her escape; during Empress it also makes her invisible, enabling full run-and-gun flanks.
- **Empress** is a chain-kill machine: each kill refreshes the timer indefinitely until she stops killing.

### Common Comm Phrases
- "Reyna eye / leer out" → Leer eyeball thrown
- "Leer's up / eye's up / don't look at the eye" → warning not to stare at the eyeball (you'll get nearsighted)
- "Shoot the eye" → destroy the Leer before it applies nearsight
- "Reyna healing / she's devouring" → Devour in progress
- "Reyna dismissed / she's intangible / she went invis" → Dismiss activated
- "Reyna ult / empress up" → Empress activated
- "She's in empress" → actively snowballing
- "Kill her in empress" → take her out before she chain-kills
- "Reyna no souls / no orbs" → she cannot use Devour or Dismiss (no kills to feed her)
- "Ult down / empress faded" → Empress timer expired

## 5. Yoru

**Origin:** Japan
**Real name:** Igara Yoru
**Lore:** A Japanese dimension-walker who can create portals, decoys, and step between realities to confuse and destroy his opponents. Trickster and assassin.
**Role emphasis:** Mind-game duelist and flank threat. The hardest duelist mechanically to play optimally. Extremely powerful in coordinated play; weaker in solo queue where teammates don't follow up on deceptions.

### Abilities

| Key | Name | Type | Cost | Charges | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C | Fakeout | Basic | 200 | 2 | Equip an echo that mimics Yoru's footstep sounds when activated. FIRE to activate and send it running forward immediately. ALT FIRE to place it dormant and activate later. USE to trigger a dormant echo. When shot by an enemy, the echo transforms into Yoru's mirror image and explodes, blinding anyone nearby. |
| Q | Blindside | Basic | 250 | 2 | Rip a dimensional fragment from reality and throw it. The fragment bounces off a surface before activating; after a 0.6s wind-up it detonates a flash that blinds anyone who sees it. Duration 1.75s. |
| E | Gatecrash | Signature | Free (150 to buy extra charge; 1 free per round; refills on 2 kills) | 2 | Equip a rift tether. FIRE to send it rolling forward. ALT FIRE to place it stationary. ACTIVATE to teleport to the tether's location. USE to trigger a fake teleport audio cue (enemies hear the teleport sound but Yoru does not move). 30s duration; reveals enemies within 4m. |
| X | Dimensional Drift | Ultimate | 8 pts | — | Equip and activate to enter Yoru's dimension. Becomes invisible and invulnerable; cannot take damage or be seen by enemies. Duration 10s. Can still be heard. Sends out decoy footsteps. Upon exiting, a flash warning plays. |

### Key Mechanics
- **Fakeout** footsteps can be sent toward a site while Yoru rotates elsewhere — classic misdirection. The clone explosion blind is a punish for enemies who shoot the decoy.
- **Blindside** bounces before popping — useful for flashing around corners without exposing self. Enemies cannot react to the bounce, only the detonation.
- **Gatecrash** has two uses: real teleport (attack unexpected angle) and fake teleport (fake audio cue causes enemies to reposition). The rift itself is visible and can be shot to deny the teleport.
- **Dimensional Drift** lets Yoru roam freely and gather intel on enemy positions while invisible. Enemies can still hear his footsteps. Entry flash on exit can be disorienting.

### Common Comm Phrases
- "Yoru clone / fakeout" → Fakeout echo deployed; may be fake footsteps
- "Clone shot" → teammate destroyed the Fakeout echo (blind detonation incoming)
- "Shoot the tether / rift on B" → Gatecrash tether visible; shoot it to deny Yoru's teleport
- "Yoru tp / teleporting / he's coming through" → real Gatecrash teleport activated
- "Fake tp / he faked it" → Yoru triggered fake teleport audio
- "Yoru flash / blindside incoming" → Blindside thrown; look away or close eyes
- "Yoru ult / he drifted / dimensional drift" → Dimensional Drift activated
- "Yoru in his ult / he's roaming" → Yoru currently invisible and gathering info
- "Pinging them / he's scouting" → Yoru in Dimensional Drift marking enemies for team
- "Yoru ult ending / he's coming out" → about to exit Dimensional Drift (flash on exit)
- "He's dumb but they'll fall for it" → teammate commenting on obvious Fakeout being used anyway

## 6. Neon

**Origin:** Philippines (Manila)
**Real name:** Tala Nicole Dimaapi Valdez
**Lore:** A young Radiant student who generates and channels electricity. Fastest agent in the game; she runs fast enough to slide and arc lightning at enemies.
**Role emphasis:** Pure speed entry. The fastest agent in Valorant. Best on maps with long corridors where her sprint speed and slide outpace enemy reaction times. Her ultimate fires a continuous lightning beam with perfect accuracy while she moves.

### Abilities

| Key | Name | Type | Cost | Charges | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C | Fast Lane | Basic | 300 | 1 | Fire two energy lines forward on the ground that extend a short distance or until hitting a surface. The lines rise into walls of static electricity that block vision and deal damage to enemies passing through. |
| Q | Relay Bolt | Basic | 200 | 1 | Throw an energy bolt that bounces once off a surface. On each surface it hits, it electrifies the ground below, creating a concussive blast that stuns enemies for 3s. |
| E | High Gear | Signature | Free (recharged by 2 kills) | 1 | Instantly channel Neon's speed for a maximum 10s sprint. ALT FIRE while charged to trigger an electric slide (one charge per High Gear activation; slide resets on kills). High Gear must first fully complete one sprint before a slide charge is granted. |
| X | Overdrive | Ultimate | 7 pts | — | Unleash full power for ~10s. Movement speed greatly increased. FIRE to channel electricity into a precise continuous lightning beam. Kills reset the Overdrive timer. |

### Key Mechanics
- **Fast Lane** walls are narrower than other smokes but come in pairs, creating a covered corridor for crossing open ground. Enemies who walk through them take damage.
- **Relay Bolt** hits the first surface and bounces to a second; both contact points produce a concussive stun. Can stun around corners.
- **High Gear** sprint is constant and dramatic — Neon is significantly faster than any other agent while sprinting. The slide is a low-profile evasive move that resets on kills.
- **Overdrive** beam has essentially zero spread while moving, turning Neon into a beam weapon that she fires while sprinting — counterintuitive and very hard to play against.

### Common Comm Phrases
- "Neon walls / fast lane out" → Fast Lane walls deployed
- "She's running / neon sprint" → High Gear active
- "She slid / she slid in" → High Gear slide used to enter site
- "Neon stun / concuss on B / relay bolt" → Relay Bolt stun active
- "Neon ult / overdrive / she's beaming" → Overdrive activated
- "She's beaming at us" → Overdrive lightning beam being fired
- "Kill her before the slide resets" → prevent Neon from getting more slides via kills
- "She's fast, don't chase" → warning not to peek Neon during sprint
- "Slide resets" → Neon killed during Overdrive; timer and slide refreshed
- "Neon ult down" → Overdrive expired or Neon killed

## 7. Iso

**Origin:** China
**Real name:** Li Zhuang
**Lore:** A hitman forged in the criminal underworld. Calm, methodical, and utterly lethal. Uses prismatic energy to create shields, walls, and pull enemies into a private arena for execution.
**Role emphasis:** Duelist designed for dueling — literally pulls enemies into 1v1s. His kit rewards mechanical skill and confidence in isolated gunfights. Kill Contract is one of the most unique ultimates in the game.

### Abilities

| Key | Name | Type | Cost | Charges | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C | Contingency | Basic | 200 | 1 | Assemble prismatic energy and fire an indestructible wall of energy that pushes forward. Blocks BOTH bullets and vision. Unlike other walls, Contingency can curve slightly and lasts 5s. |
| Q | Undercut | Basic | 300 | 1 | Throw a molecular bolt forward that passes through walls and terrain. All agents touched are inflicted with Fragile (increased damage taken) and Suppressed (abilities disabled) for 4s. |
| E | Double Tap | Signature | Free (recharged after 2 kills) | 1 | Start a focus timer; after completing, enter a flow state for 12s. During this state, killing or damaging a downed enemy generates an energy orb above them. Shoot the orb to gain a shield that absorbs one full instance of damage. |
| X | Kill Contract | Ultimate | 7 pts | — | Hurl a column of energy across the map, pulling you and the first enemy it hits into an interdimensional arena. 1v1 duel to the death; winner exits the arena with 100 HP. 15s time limit. Iso now starts Kill Contract with Double Tap active (post-patch 10.04). |

### Key Mechanics
- **Contingency** is the only wall in Valorant that blocks bullets entirely in both directions — it can protect from Operator shots and allow safe pushes. Slightly curved path.
- **Undercut** passes through walls — it can Suppress enemies behind cover, disabling their abilities at a critical moment. Fragile debuff makes them take increased damage.
- **Double Tap** orb requires actively shooting the orb after a kill; the orb is briefly visible above the downed enemy. Shield blocks one instance of any damage.
- **Kill Contract** removes both Iso and the targeted enemy from the round temporarily. If Iso wins the 1v1, he returns; if he loses, it's a 4v5. The arena is a private dimension.

### Common Comm Phrases
- "Iso wall / contingency / wall up" → Contingency deployed
- "His wall's up, don't peek through it" → warning about bullet-blocking
- "Iso bolt / undercut incoming" → Undercut molecular bolt thrown
- "He suppressed / suppressed on B" → Undercut hit; enemy abilities disabled for 4s
- "Fragile / he's fragile" → enemy under Undercut Fragile debuff — deal more damage
- "Iso shield / he has shield" → Double Tap orb consumed and shield is active
- "Shoot the orb" → teammate spotted Double Tap orb above downed enemy; shoot it
- "Iso ult / kill contract / he's pulling someone" → Kill Contract activated
- "He pulled [name] / [name] got contracted" → specific enemy pulled into arena
- "Win the 1v1 / he's dueling" → Kill Contract arena in progress
- "Iso lost the duel / he's dead in contract" → Iso eliminated inside arena
- "He came back" → Iso won Kill Contract; returns to map at 100 HP

## 8. Waylay

**Origin:** Thailand (Bangkok)
**Real name:** Nattaporn Sumalee (unofficial; codename "Waylay")
**Lore:** Thailand's prismatic Radiant who transforms into pure light to dash across the battlefield. First new space-taking duelist since Jett and Raze at launch. Designed for approachable but high-skill mobility.
**Role emphasis:** Space-taking duelist with two dashes and a safe-return beacon. Can contest aggressive early positions similarly to Jett/Raze, but Refract gives her a guaranteed escape route back to beacon.

### Abilities

| Key | Name | Type | Cost | Charges | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| C | Saturate | Basic | 300 | 1 | Throw a cluster of light that explodes on contact with the ground, Hindering all nearby players. The Hinder debuff reduces movement speed, fire rate, recoil recovery, equip time, reload speed, and jump speed. No vision blur. |
| Q | Lightspeed | Basic | 300 | 2 (both used together) | EQUIP to prepare a burst of speed. FIRE to dash forward twice. ALT FIRE to dash once. Only the first dash can travel upward. Both dashes activate as a sequence; you do not individually control each one. |
| E | Refract | Signature | Free (recharged by 2 kills) | 1 | Instantly place a beacon of light on the floor. At any point, REACTIVATE to instantly travel back to the beacon as a mote of pure light — invulnerable during transit. |
| X | Convergent Paths | Ultimate | 8 pts | — | Focus prismatic power and FIRE to create an afterimage of yourself projecting a beam of light forward. After a brief delay, grants Waylay a speed boost and expands the beam outward, Hindering all players caught in its radius. |

### Key Mechanics
- **Saturate** Hinder is a comprehensive slowdown — it reduces nearly every action the enemy can take, not just movement. No blinding effect; purely a mobility/combat debuff.
- **Lightspeed** gives two forward dashes but they fire as a sequence rather than independent use. The upward angle on the first dash allows creative aerial entries, similar to Jett but more committed.
- **Refract** is the beacon system: place it before peeking or entering, then recall if the trade goes wrong. Invulnerable while traveling back. The beacon is visible on the ground.
- **Convergent Paths** zones a large area; the expanding beam pushes enemies into slower reaction states, making them easier to hit. Waylay also gains speed to capitalize on the zoned enemies.

### Note on spelling: The ability "Lightspeed" is one word in the official Valorant Wiki. Some community sources and announcement materials spell it "Light Speed" (two words). The canonical Wiki spelling is **Lightspeed**.

### Common Comm Phrases
- "Waylay slow / saturate / she slowed B" → Saturate thrown; enemies in zone are hindered
- "Hindered / they're slowed" → Saturate debuff active on enemies
- "Waylay dashing / she's dashing" → Lightspeed in use
- "She went up / aerial dash" → Lightspeed first-dash upward trajectory used
- "Waylay beacon / beacon down" → Refract beacon placed; she will likely recall to it
- "Shoot the beacon" → destroy Refract beacon to deny her escape
- "She recalled / she went back" → Refract reactivated; Waylay teleported back to beacon
- "Waylay ult / convergent paths / she's converging" → Convergent Paths activated
- "Beam expanding / zone out" → expanding Hinder beam from Convergent Paths; move out
- "She got speed from ult" → Waylay speed boost portion of Convergent Paths

## Cross-Agent Duelist Comm Patterns

### Ability Tracking Template
Short and declarative. Ability name or slang word + direction/location.

Examples:
- "Jett no dash" (critical — she's exposed)
- "Raze ult pushing mid"
- "Phoenix ult down" (safe to engage Phoenix now)
- "Reyna empress'd up"
- "Yoru ulting, he's invisible"
- "Iso contracted Drx's Jett"
- "Waylay beacon on CT, she'll recall there"

### Dueling Coordination Phrases
- "Entry on my flash" → wait for initiator/duelist flash then push
- "Swing on their contact" → push when the entry makes contact with the enemy
- "Take it / duel it" → permission for the duelist to take the aggressive fight
- "Back off / disengage" → too risky; don't take the duel
- "Trade if he dies" → if the entry goes down, the second person immediately swings
- "Don't peek, they're stacked" → defender-side info; too many enemies at angle
- "Space is taken / site is clear" → entry succeeded; come in
- "Give space / pull back" → do not push; reassess

### Economy / Utility State
- "Ult up" → ultimate is charged and ready this round
- "Ult one away / need one orb" → one more orb needed for ultimate
- "Save your dash / don't burn it" → specific Jett instruction to preserve Tailwind
- "She's tilted / throwing" → read that an enemy duelist is playing emotionally reckless
- "She needs kills / no orbs" → Reyna who hasn't gotten kills has no Dismiss/Devour

## Status Effects Relevant to Duelists

| Effect | Source Agent | What It Does |
|---|---|---|
| Nearsight | Reyna (Leer), others | Severe vision reduction — only see directly in front |
| Blind / Flash | Jett (Cloudburst barely), Phoenix (Curveball), Yoru (Blindside, Fakeout) | White screen; cannot aim |
| Hinder | Waylay (Saturate, Convergent Paths) | Reduces movement speed, fire rate, reload, equip, jump |
| Concuss / Stun | Neon (Relay Bolt) | Movement and ability disruption |

| Intangible | Reyna (Dismiss) | Cannot take damage; passes through effects |
| Invulnerable | Yoru (Dimensional Drift), Waylay (Refract transit), Reyna (Dismiss) | Cannot be harmed |
