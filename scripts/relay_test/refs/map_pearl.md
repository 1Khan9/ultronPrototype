# Valorant — Pearl Map Reference

**Status:** Current as of June 2026  
**Pool status:** Active in competitive rotation (Episode 9 / Act 3, 2026)  
**Defining feature:** **No dynamic elements** — no teleporters, ropes, doors, zip lines, or mechanical hooks. The only map in the pool with zero map mechanics. All rotations are on foot; timing is everything.  
**Setting:** Underwater geodesic dome encasing a portion of **Lisbon, Portugal** on **Omega Earth** (the Mirror Earth dimension). Climate collapse has submerged the old city; Kingdom Industries used Radianite-infused polycarbonate domes to preserve the district. Looking out from B site you can see the crumbling ruins of old Lisbon beyond the glass. Portuguese murals line the walls; Portuguese music plays during loading. The locale is dense Mediterranean architecture — plazas, restaurants, a nightclub (Odyssey), records shops, a water feature room, and layered corridors.  
**Bomb sites:** A Site and B Site  
**Map bias:** Slight defender advantage (~52–53% CT win rate). B Main is one of the longest committed-entry corridors in the game, heavily punishing uncoordinated A/B pushes.  
**Released:** Episode 5 Act 1, Patch 5.0 (June 22, 2022)  
**Notable patch changes:**
- **Patch 5.06:** A Art layout adjusted; Mid Top wall pushed inward to remove a 50-50 check and ease defender challenges.
- **Patch 6.11 (June 6, 2023):** B Upper Ramp platform reduced + slope behind shrunk to jump-box; B Screen made shorter and wider with impenetrable blocks added behind it; B Hall new cubby added (left entry from Screen side, wider entry width); B Tower Pillar thickened to give defenders more holding space and force attackers to clear a larger area.

---

## Map Overview

Pearl is a **standard two-site, three-lane map** without shortcuts. The three lanes are:
1. **A lane** — Attacker Spawn → A Restaurant → A Main → A Site
2. **Mid lane** — Attacker Spawn → Mid Top / Mid Shops → Mid Plaza → Mid Connector / Mid Doors → B Link or A Art
3. **B lane** — Attacker Spawn → B Club → B Main → B Site

Mid Plaza is the strategic hub of the map. Controlling Mid Doors and Mid Plaza opens flanking angles into both sites simultaneously. The team that wins mid typically wins the round; rotations from A to B (or vice versa) for defenders are long and punishing if mid is given up.

**No ultimate orb confirmed locations:** One ultimate orb is on the attacker side of A Main; one is in Mid Plaza (central area).

**Layout sketch:**
```
[Attacker Spawn / Amphitheatre]
        |            |          |          |
   A Restaurant   Mid Shops   Mid Top   B Club
        |              \         /         |
      A Main          Mid Plaza           B Main
        |                  |                |
      A Link          Mid Connector      B Ramp → B Upper Ramp
        |             /        \              |
      A Art      A Link       B Link       B Screen
        |                         \           |
      A Site                    B Hall       B Site
     (A Café, A Dugout,                  (B Tower, B Tunnel)
      A Flowers, A Secret)
                                [Defender Spawn]
                               /     |      |     \
                         A Secret  A Flowers  Def Water  Def Records
                                              |              |
                                         Mid Connector    B Tunnel → B Tower
```

## A Site Area

### Approach Routes

| Callout | Alt Names | Description |
|---|---|---|
| A Restaurant | Restaurant, Rest, A Entrance | Named for the restaurant/café building visible on the attacker-side approach. One of the two attacker spawn exits toward A site. Wide corridor leading into A Main. Contains defender pockets and the first angles attackers face leaving spawn. |
| A Main | A Long, Main | Primary attacker entry corridor to A site. Long push from A Restaurant. Features deep defender pockets on the inner walls that can be wallbanged. Contains an **ultimate orb** on the curved section of the path. Wallbangable wooden crates/boxes at the curve are frequently used for cover. Tight and punishing for uncoordinated attacks; smokes on the angle at A Link entry are mandatory for safe entry. |
| A Art | Art, Art Room | Area reached from Mid Plaza via Mid Top. L-shaped corridor lined with colourful artwork and murals. Connects mid to A Link and then to A site. Attackers route through Art to flank A site from mid or cut off a rotating defender. Defenders use Art to hold mid aggression or fall off after a post-plant. "A Art" is considered a challenging initial push for attackers since defenders often pre-position there from spawn. Contains boxes defenders use for cover and wall openings for ability lineups. |
| A Link | Link | The chokepoint corridor that opens from A Main (and A Art from mid) into A site. Defenders frequently play close angles at A Link or hold with an Operator from deep. Attackers need to clear multiple angles simultaneously — the Link entrance, Café corner, and Dugout — before committing to site. |

### On-Site Positions

| Callout | Alt Names | Description |
|---|---|---|
| A Site | Site, Bomb Site A | The bomb plant zone. Relatively small and claustrophobic by Valorant standards. Flanked by A Café on one side and A Dugout on the other. Default plant is in the open area near A Café/Vending Machines; alternative plants in Dugout or behind cover toward A Flowers. |
| A Café | Café, A Cafe, Vending Machines | L-shaped building/corner structure on A site. Primary spike plant location — most attackers plant behind or beside it ("Vending Machines" / "behind Café"). Defenders can play on top of the Café structure or behind it for close angles. Can be boosted onto from the wall adjacent to A Dugout. |
| A Dugout | Dugout | Sunken/recessed area on A site, usually behind or beside the main planting box. Deep cover that defenders use to remain invisible to attackers entering via A Link. Clearing Dugout is mandatory before committing to plant. Attackers use it as a post-plant position facing A Link. |
| A Flowers | Flowers | Defender-controlled area adjacent to A site near the defender-side path. Features a small window connecting to A Secret. Defenders fall back to Flowers when the site is overwhelmed; attackers use it as a post-plant position or to cut off rotations. The window between Flowers and Secret is usable by both sides for flanks. |
| A Secret | Secret | Area on the defender side adjacent to A Flowers. Accessed from Defender Spawn. Contains a window connecting to A Flowers. Defenders use Secret + Flowers as their primary A-side defensive setup. Attackers who hack into the defender side via Mid Connector can threaten Secret as a flank. |

## B Site Area

### Approach Routes

| Callout | Alt Names | Description |
|---|---|---|
| B Club | Club, Odyssey | Named for the "Odyssey" nightclub entrance visible in the attacker spawn corridor on the B side. The attacker spawn exit leading toward B Main. Tight passage connecting Attacker Spawn to B Main. |

One of the longest committed-entry corridors in Valorant — defenders can set up extended Operator angles from B Ramp down B Main. Features wallbangable stacked boxes/crates. Being caught in B Main without smokes is a severe disadvantage; Viper Wall cutting across B Main is the standard defensive tool. |

Can be wallbanged — the original screen attached to Ramp is penetrable. |
| B Screen | Screen | The large cover screen/pillar on B site facing B Main. Post-Patch 6.11: made shorter and wider with impenetrable blocks added behind it to prevent spam kills through it. Defenders use B Screen to hold the B Main angle. Both sides can reach the Screen position; a common sniper battle location at the start of rounds. |
| B Link | B Mid Link, Link | The attacking route into B site from Mid Plaza (via Mid Doors and Mid Shops). Requires Mid Plaza control before committing. A difficult push lane featuring multiple lurking positions and wallbangable cubbies along the corridor. Defenders play B Link to deny mid-to-B rotations by attackers. Post-Patch 6.11: B Link / Hall entry received a new cubby and wider entry, creating additional angles for both sides. |

## Key Strategic Notes

**No mechanics = timing is everything:** Pearl has no teleporters, ropes, doors, or elevators. All rotations are on foot. Defenders caught rotating A-to-B (or vice versa) without mid control will be timed out of the round. This makes mid control and early information more valuable than on almost any other map.

**Mid control priority:** Mid Doors → Mid Plaza → (A Art OR Mid Connector) is the attacker flowchart. If attackers win mid, they can execute A from two angles (A Main + A Art from link) or push B from two angles (B Main + B Link). A team that cedes mid has only single-entry routes on each site.

**B site attack:** B Main is punishing without utility. Standard execute: smoke B Tower + B Hall, flash B Screen area, push with 3–4 players up B Main while one clears B Ramp angle. Viper Wall across B Main to cut the Operator angle is the most common opener.

**A site attack:** A Main push requires A Art control from mid OR a straight A Main push with smokes on A Link. The standard A execute smokes A Link entry and the far A Café corner; a flash through A Link forces defenders off deep angles. A Art flankers from mid add a second entry vector.

**Defender rotations:** Defender Water → Mid Connector is the A-to-B rotate path (faster than going all the way around through spawn if mid is unsafe). Defender Records → B Tunnel → B Tower or B Hall is the standard B-side setup. A-side defenders use A Secret → A Flowers positions.

**Post-plant on A:** Plant behind A Café/Vending Machines; hold from A Dugout facing A Link, or from A Flowers facing the site. A Secret window angle used to deny defuse.

**Post-plant on B:** Plant behind Radianite boxes facing B Ramp; one player holds B Ramp, one watches B Hall. B Link post-plant crossfire (player on B Link) is a strong secondary hold.

## Ability Lineup Notes (Key Agents)

### Sova — Pearl Recon Bolt Spots
- **A Main Recon:** Launched from Attacker Spawn / near A Restaurant, bounced to land deep in A Main revealing defenders in pockets.
- **B Site Recon from B Club:** Fired into B site to reveal B Hall, B Tower, or B Screen positions.
- **B Main Recon from Mid:** Fired from Mid Top over the wall to land in B Main and reveal defenders.

### KAY/O — Pearl Fragment Spots
- **B Hall Fragment:** Through the wall from B Link to explode inside B Hall — standard B execute opener.
- **A Link Fragment:** Thrown from A Main to clear A Link close angles.
- **Mid Doors Fragment:** Suppresses defenders holding Mid Doors without exposing KAY/O to their crossfire.

### Killjoy — Pearl Utility Placements
- **Turret on Mid Shops / Mid Top:** Watches mid and gives information on approaching enemies.
- **Nanoswarm on B Ramp:** Forces defenders off Ramp during B site execute.
- **Nanoswarm on A Link:** Denies defuse through A Link during post-plant.
- **Lockdown on B Site:** Post-execute, forces a site clear or spike defuse denial.

### Viper — Pearl Utility Placements
- **Viper Wall across B Main:** The definitive B Main utility. Cuts the entire corridor, denying Operator angles and forcing attackers/defenders to commit to one side.
- **Poison Cloud on B Hall:** Blocks the B Hall angle during B site executes.
- **Viper's Pit on B Site:** Post-plant ultimate placing defenders in a double-controller layer they cannot defuse through.
