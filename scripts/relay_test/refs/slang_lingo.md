# Valorant Community Slang, Shotcalling Lingo & Competitive Terminology

*Reference document for the Ultron relay test corpus. Sources: gametree.me, diamondlobby.com, esportsdriven.com, wecoach.gg, hotspawn.com, boosteria.org, valorfeed.gg, thegamer.com, earlygame.com, redbull.com, whatifgaming.com, prosettings.net, metabot.gg. Verified June 2026.*

---

## 1. CORE COMBAT SLANG

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Ace** | One player eliminates all five enemies in a single round. |
| **Team Ace** | Each teammate gets exactly one kill in a round with no casualties on either side. |
| **Frag / Pick** | A kill. "Entry frag" = first kill of the round opening the site. |
| **Top frag** | Player with the most kills on the team or in the match. |
| **Bottom frag** | Player with the fewest kills. |
| **Carry** | Player doing the majority of the work; far more kills/impact than teammates. |
| **Clutch** | A player wins a round alone vs. multiple enemies. Also: anything clinched under extreme pressure. |
| **Dink / Dinked / Gooshed** | A headshot that does NOT kill — the target survives at very low HP. Named after the in-game hit sound. |
| **Lit** | Enemy has taken significant damage and is close to death. ("He's lit" / "tagged 80" = 80 HP remaining.) |
| **One shot** | Enemy is at low enough HP that any bullet kills them. Interchangeable with "lit" in many contexts. |
| **One-tap** | Killing an enemy with a single bullet, typically a headshot (Vandal, Guardian, Sheriff). |
| **One-deag** | Killing with a single headshot from the Sheriff (the pistol modeled after the Desert Eagle / "Deagle"). |
| **Collateral** | Killing two enemies with one bullet or ability. |
| **Headshot (HS)** | Shot hitting an enemy's head. |
| **Aimpunch** | Screen shake and aim disruption caused by receiving incoming fire. |
| **Whiff / Whiffing** | Missing shots you should have hit; particularly embarrassing missed shots. |
| **Flick** | Rapidly snapping the crosshair onto a target and firing, often for a one-shot kill. |
| **Tracking** | Keeping the crosshair on a moving target across time. |
| **Counterstrafe** | Tapping the opposite movement key to instantly halt momentum, allowing accurate shots mid-movement. |
| **Prefire** | Shooting at a spot before actually seeing the enemy, based on prediction of their position. |
| **Wallbang / Penned** | Shooting through a penetrable wall/surface to damage or kill a hidden enemy. |
| **No scope** | Firing a sniper rifle (Marshal or Operator) without using ADS. |
| **Crouch spray** | Crouching while firing sustained automatic fire to control recoil. |
| **Run and gun** | Firing while moving; generally inaccurate but can work at very close range. |
| **Hip fire** | Shooting without aiming down sights (no right-click zoom). |
| **Double swing** | Two players peek around a corner simultaneously to overwhelm a single defender. |
| **Exit frag** | Defenders killing retreating attackers after the spike detonates or after a failed execute. |
| **Feed / Feeding** | Dying repeatedly without delivering value; giving the enemy kills and ultimate orbs. |
| **Int / Inting** | "Intentional feeding" — deliberately dying or playing as if you want the enemy to win. |
| **Refrag** | Immediately killing the enemy who just killed your teammate; also called a trade. |

## 2. POSITIONING & MOVEMENT TERMS

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Angle / Holding angles** | The line of sight a defender covers; "holding angles" = waiting at a corner for an enemy to walk in. |
| **Angle advantage** | The positional benefit where the player farther from the angle sees the target first. |
| **Off-angle** | An unexpected position that is NOT the standard angle; surprises enemies whose crosshairs are pre-aimed elsewhere. |
| **Peeker's advantage** | The network/latency effect where the player peeking around a corner sees the defender before the defender sees them. |
| **Jiggle peek** | Rapidly strafe-peeking in and out of cover to gather info or bait shots without committing. |
| **Shoulder peek** | Briefly exposing just the shoulder to bait enemy fire, revealing their position without dying. |
| **Jump peek** | Jumping from cover to briefly spot enemies, harder to hit due to vertical movement. |
| **Wide peek** | Stepping far out from cover when peeking, giving more screen real estate to react before the defender can adjust. |
| **Dry peek** | Checking an angle without any ability support; raw mechanical peek. |
| **Poppin' swing** | Aggressively committing to a wide swing to catch an angle-holder off guard. |
| **Lurk** | A solo player quietly separating from the team to flank, gather info, or hunt isolated enemies. |
| **Anchor / Anchoring** | A defender holding a site alone to delay attackers and buy time for teammates to rotate. |
| **Flank** | Approaching enemies from behind or from an unexpected side. |
| **Rat / Ratting** | Hiding in an unconventional or extremely passive spot to avoid enemies; can be a compliment (clever) or insult (cowardly). |
| **Camp / Camping** | Sitting in one spot for an extended period waiting for enemies to walk into you. |
| **Shift walk** | Moving while holding Shift — silent footsteps, slower speed. |
| **Bunnyhop / B-hop** | Jumping repeatedly (combined with mouse air-strafing) to maintain momentum; harder to execute in Valorant than CS but knife-running still applicable. |
| **Heaven** | Any elevated platform or high ground position on a site (generic callout name). |
| **Hell** | The area directly beneath Heaven. |
| **Elbow** | A bent corridor creating a right-angle corner, used in map callouts. |
| **Cubby** | A recessed nook or small alcove where a player can tuck away. |
| **Long** | A wide, open lane that extends deep into the map; favors rifle/sniper play. |
| **Short** | A close-range corridor or tight entrance. |
| **Mid** | The middle-lane area connecting the two sites; often contested for map control. |
| **Main** | The primary wide entrance to a bomb site. |
| **Back site** | The deepest area of the site, closest to the defender's spawn. |
| **Window** | A sightline cutout in a wall (generic callout or specific map feature). |
| **Lobby** | Defender-side pre-round safe area; often also a map callout for specific open areas. |
| **Link** | A connector path between two map lanes. |

## 3. TACTICAL SHOTCALLING TERMS

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Callout** | Verbal communication about an enemy's position (e.g., "One B long, close left"). |
| **IGL** | In-game leader — the player making strategic calls and directing round-to-round execution. |
| **Strat** | Short for strategy; the plan for a round. |
| **Default** | An attacker-side setup where no one commits to a site early; instead players spread and gather information. |
| **Default plant** | The most common spike-plant location on a given site. |
| **Fake / Faking** | Committing utility and presence to one site to draw rotations, then pivoting to hit the other site. |
| **Rotate** | Moving from one site to the other in response to enemy actions. |
| **Hold rotate** | Calling for a teammate NOT to rotate yet, to avoid exposing a flank or reacting too early. |
| **Stack** | Stacking 4 or 5 players on one site (defense) or committing full team force to one lane (attack). |
| **Split** | Hitting a site from two different entry points simultaneously to overwhelm defenders. |
| **Rush** | Fast, committed push to a site with no elaborate slow setup. |
| **Contact** | Moving into contested space and engaging enemies without deploying utility first. |
| **Explode** | Hit a site fast using layered utility in quick succession. |
| **Scale** | Take space step by step, trading positions, rather than all-in rushing. |
| **Reset** | Stop the current push, regroup, and re-engage with a new plan. |
| **Play slow** | Cautious approach — take time, gather information, let utility/clock work. |
| **Play for picks** | Spreading out and playing passively to kill isolated enemies rather than committing to an execute. |
| **Play for time** | Defender strategy: survive, delay, and run the clock to starve the attackers of plant time. |
| **Pinch** | Attacking a position from two sides at once, trapping enemies. |
| **Crossfire** | Two teammates covering the same angle from opposite sides so the enemy faces fire from two directions. |
| **Trade** | Killing the enemy who just killed your teammate, immediately. |
| **Ability trading** | Forcing enemies to exhaust their abilities to neutralize yours; a parity exchange. |
| **Bait** | Using a teammate (or ability) to draw enemy fire, exposing their position for the bait-setter or others to punish. |
| **Anti-flash** | Positioning behind cover when a flash lands, then swinging after it expires. |
| **Drill / Execute** | A coordinated, pre-planned site take with assigned utility usage in sequence. |
| **Swing on my contact** | Wait until I engage the enemy; then you swing to trade. |
| **Flash in three, two, one** | Countdown to synchronize a push with a flash. |
| **Tap spike, I swing** | Retake coordination: one player briefly starts defusing to bait defenders out; partner punishes them. |
| **Planting for [location]** | Planting the spike oriented toward a specific post-plant position the team will hold from. |
| **Plant for main / plant for CT** | Directional plant call designating the spike's location and defensive angle advantage. |
| **Play crossfire (post-plant)** | After planting, split into two separated angles to catch defenders from both directions. |
| **Half it** | Abbreviated version of "half defuse" — tap the spike briefly for a fake. |
| **Save exit** | Protect economy by avoiding a pointless fight at round end; keep your rifle. |
| **Information** | Knowledge of enemy positions or utility status; intel. |
| **Late** | Intentionally slow timing to burn enemy utility, stretch the clock, or bait rotations. |
| **Contact only** | Begin the push only after enemies are spotted or engaged; no blind commit. |
| **Defaulting** | See Default above; also used as a verb: "We're defaulting this round." |
| **Anchor with info** | The anchor player should provide position updates to the team, not just survive passively. |
| **Don't rotate away from information** | Don't abandon a position until you know it's safe; keep an alive player reading the enemy. |

## 4. UTILITY / ABILITY LANGUAGE

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Smoke** | Any ability creating a vision-blocking cloud (Brimstone Sky Smoke, Omen Dark Cover, Viper Poison Cloud / Toxic Screen, Astra Nebula, Harbor Cove, Miks Waveform). |
| **One-way smoke** | A smoke placed so one side can see through it while the other cannot; requires specific elevation/gap. |
| **Wall** | A smoke ability that creates a long linear barrier splitting sight lines (Viper Toxic Screen, Harbor High Tide). |
| **Molly** | Community slang for any area-denial lingering-damage ability; from "Molotov." In Valorant: Brimstone Incendiary, Phoenix Hot Hands, Viper Snake Bite, Gekko Mosh Pit. |
| **Flash** | Any ability that blinds/disoriates enemies: Reyna Leer, Phoenix Curveball, Breach Flashpoint, Skye Guiding Light, KAY/O FLASH/drive, Yoru Blindside. |
| **Drone** | Remote-controlled reconnaissance ability: Sova Owl Drone, Tejo Stealth Drone. |
| **Dog** | Community nickname for Skye's Trailblazer (tiger cub ability). |
| **Roomba / Car** | Community nickname for Raze's Boom Bot. |
| **Lineup** | A specific player position + aim angle combination guaranteeing an ability lands at a precise map location. |
| **Jumpthrow** | Jumping and releasing an ability (smoke, grenade) simultaneously to achieve lineups that require specific launch arcs. |
| **Utility** | General term for all agent abilities. |
| **CD / Cooldown** | An ability is on cooldown and unavailable temporarily. |
| **Ult / Ultimate** | The X-key agent ability, charged through kills, deaths, winning rounds, or picking up orbs. |
| **Orb** | A glowing sphere on the map that grants one ultimate charge when picked up. |
| **He used dash** | Specific callout: the enemy used their movement ability (usually Jett Tailwind, Neon High Gear/dash). Now they can't escape. |
| **No flash** | The enemy initiator has no flash available; safe to push. |
| **Smoke fading** | The smoke is about to expire; prepare to push or brace for contact. |
| **Drone out** | Enemy has an active recon drone scanning the area. |
| **Trap here** | A sentinel ability (Cypher Tripwire, Killjoy Alarmbot/Nanoswarm, Deadlock Sonic Sensor, Vyse Razorvine, Veto Interceptor/Chokehold) is deployed in this location. |
| **Molly on spike** | An area-denial ability is covering the spike, delaying or preventing defuse. |
| **Wall broken** | A barrier ability (Sage Barrier Orb, Deadlock Barrier Mesh, Killjoy Lockdown radius wall) has been destroyed. |
| **Rez / Res** | Sage's Resurrection ultimate — reviving a dead teammate. |
| **Res me** | Requesting Sage to use Resurrection on you. |
| **Dog** | Skye's Trailblazer; "dog's out" = Skye is using Trailblazer to scout. |
| **Seize** | Fade's tethering ability. Being "seized" means CC'd in place. |
| **Grav** | Deadlock GravNet — traps enemies so they can't jump or move freely. |
| **KO'd** | KAY/O's ZERO/point knife suppresses enemies, removing their ability to use abilities. Being "KO'd" = suppressed. |
| **Suppressed** | Enemy/ally cannot use abilities due to KAY/O knife. |

## 5. ECONOMY TERMS

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Credits / Creds** | The in-game currency; earned by winning rounds, kills, planting/defusing spike. |
| **Pistol round** | The first round of each half (attack and defense sides). Everyone starts with 800 credits; can only buy pistols. |
| **Bonus round** | Round immediately following a pistol round win; team keeps guns from previous round while still able to purchase limited upgrades. Sometimes called "bonus" or "second round." |
| **Full buy** | Purchasing a full loadout: Vandal/Phantom/Operator, heavy armor (150-shield), and full abilities. Requires ~4,000+ credits. |
| **Half buy / Light buy** | Purchasing a partial loadout — a cheaper rifle (Bulldog/Guardian), light armor, and some abilities. Preserves credits. |
| **Force buy / Force** | Spending all available credits despite not having enough for a proper full buy; gambling on a round win. |
| **Eco / Eco round** | Deliberately spending very little (usually buying nothing or only a pistol) to save credits for a future full buy. |
| **Half eco** | Spending a small amount while keeping 2,000+ credits for next round. |
| **Full save** | Spending nothing at all; don't buy a weapon, don't buy abilities. |
| **Save** | Surviving the round and keeping your rifle by avoiding fights when the round is lost. "Save your gun." |
| **Anti-eco** | Playing against an enemy team that is on an eco round; buying smart to maximize economic advantage while minimizing risks. |
| **Thrifty** | The post-round medal for winning while economically outgunned. Community slang: winning a round against a full-buy team while on an eco or half-buy. |
| **Glass cannon** | Buying an expensive weapon (Operator, Vandal) without purchasing armor. |
| **Bonus round** | See above; also: a round where you can keep SMGs/shotguns from a pistol win rather than full-buying. |
| **Drop** | One teammate buying a weapon and giving it to another: "Drop me Vandal." |
| **AK** | Slang (from CS) referring to the Vandal. |
| **AWP** | Slang (from CS) referring to the Operator. |
| **Pocket Guardian** | Buying a Guardian during a low-economy round to punch above your budget. Historically also associated with Chamber's Headhunter. |
| **RR** | Rank Rating — the visible points (0–100 per division) tracking progress between ranks. |
| **MMR** | Matchmaking Rating — hidden skill rating influencing who you're matched against and how much RR you gain or lose. |

## 6. POST-PLANT / RETAKE LANGUAGE

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Post-plant** | The period after the spike has been planted; attackers try to protect it, defenders try to defuse. |
| **Spike down / Spike planted** | Callout confirming the spike has been planted. |
| **Spike spotted** | Callout confirming you know where the spike is planted. |
| **Retake** | Defenders regrouping and coordinating to reclaim a site and defuse the spike. |
| **Default plant** | Planting at the most common, statistically expected location on the site. |
| **Plant for [main/CT/elbow]** | Directional plant call; the spike is oriented so the attacker holding [location] has the angle advantage to protect it. |
| **Safe plant** | Planting the spike in a protected position behind cover, minimizing defender defuse angles. |
| **Ninja defuse** | Stealthily defusing the spike without clearing the site or alerting attackers; high-risk, high-reward. |
| **Fake defuse** | Starting the defuse animation to bait attackers out of hiding, then stopping and trading the swing. |
| **Stick** | Commit to defusing and don't stop; "stick it" = ride out the defuse. |
| **Tap spike** | Briefly start the defuse (instant stop) to create fake-defuse pressure. |
| **Playing time** | Post-plant attacker strategy: avoid fighting, stay alive, let the spike count down. |
| **Molly on spike** | An incendiary is placed directly on or near the spike to prevent safe defuse. |
| **Wall the site** | Using smoke/wall abilities to block key sightlines during a retake. |

## 7. ROUND FLOW / GAME STATE LANGUAGE

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Entry** | Command or role: the player going in first. "Entry in three" = coordinate a site take. |
| **Execute** | A full coordinated attack on a site using utility in sequence. |
| **Fake execute** | Committing utility and at least one body to a site before pulling back; designed to trigger rotations. |
| **Split push** | See "Split" in Section 3; attacking from two lanes simultaneously. |
| **Mid control** | Owning or contesting the middle of the map, which opens rotation paths and information. |
| **Give space** | Defenders retreating from a choke, conceding map territory. |
| **They gave space / Mid is open** | Callout: no resistance was encountered; enemy ceded this area. |
| **They rotated off** | Defenders have left this area and are moving elsewhere. |
| **They're walking** | Enemy is shift-walking (silent push); listen for footsteps approaching. |
| **They're rushing** | Enemy is committing at full speed. |
| **Rush B / Rush A** | All-in, no-utility fast push to a specific site. |
| **Slow push** | Cautious advance using utility to take ground incrementally. |
| **Remake** | Match restart (first few rounds, usually due to a disconnect); no RR penalty. |
| **FF / Forfeit** | Vote to end the match early; teams can surrender after a certain number of rounds. |
| **OT / Overtime** | Extra rounds when the match is tied after regulation (12–12). |
| **Pistol won** | Team won the opening pistol round. |
| **9-3 curse** | The Valorant phenomenon where teams with a 9-3 halftime lead frequently collapse and lose; heavy meme. |

## 9. PERFORMANCE / SKILL SLANG

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Cracked** | Playing at an exceptionally high mechanical level; insane aim, reflexes, game sense. "He's cracked." |
| **Diff** | Short for "difference"; used to say one player outclassed the opponent in the same role. "Jett diff." / "Initiator diff." |
| **Aimlabs** | The free aim-training program (Aimlabs on Steam); used as slang to suggest someone needs to practice their aim. "Go back to Aimlabs." |
| **Flicking like a god / Cracked flick** | Describing an unusually fast, accurate crosshair snap. |
| **Hard stuck** | Unable to rank up despite effort; the ranking system's MMR has equalized with your visible rank. |
| **Elo hell** | A lower-rank tier where low-quality teammates and smurfs make climbing feel impossible. |
| **Smurf** | A high-ranked player on a secondary (alternate) account deliberately playing in lower-ranked lobbies. |
| **Boosted** | A player who is ranked higher than their actual skill level due to boosting (a high-ranked player playing on their account). |
| **One trick / OTP (One-Trick Pony)** | A player who exclusively plays one agent and rarely deviates. Can be impressive or a liability. |
| **Instalock** | Immediately locking an agent in agent select without considering team composition. Usually means a duelist. |
| **Battle Sage** | Derogatory: a Sage player who ignores healing/walls in favor of fragging aggressively. |
| **Pocket Sage** | An overly passive Sage who plays exclusively for the team, never fragging. |
| **Top frag** | Player with the most kills. |
| **Derank** | Intentionally losing to lower your rank, often to play against weaker opponents. |
| **Inting** | See Section 1: feeding intentionally. |
| **Griefing** | Any action deliberately hindering your own team: blocking, using utility on allies, etc. |
| **Throw / Throwing** | Losing a round or match that was winnable due to mistakes, overconfidence, or sabotage. |
| **Tilt / Tilting** | Emotional frustration degrading performance; making irrational calls or plays due to anger. |
| **Toxic** | Extremely negative in-game behavior: insults, blame, throwing, griefing. |
| **Rage quit** | Leaving a match mid-game due to frustration. |
| **Rage hack** | Using cheats in frustration after losing. |
| **9-3 curse** | Teams at 9-3 at halftime frequently collapse and lose 11-13 or go to overtime; community superstition/meme. |
| **AFK** | Away From Keyboard — player is not active; can result in an AFK penalty. |
| **Deafened** | Status effect from certain abilities (Miks M-pulse, Veto Chokehold, Deadlock) — enemy cannot hear footsteps or ability sounds. |

## 15. TACTICAL COMMUNICATION TEMPLATES

These are shorthand sentence structures used in real shotcalling:

| Template | Usage |
|----------|-------|
| `[Count] [Location] [Status]` | "Two B main, close." / "One Heaven, lit." |
| `He's lit / He's one shot` | Enemy is nearly dead — push NOW. |
| `[Ability] out / [Ability] fading` | "Smoke fading mid." / "Drone out short." |
| `Rotate now` | Commit to other site immediately. |
| `Hold rotate` | Don't leave yet; wait for confirmation. |
| `Flash in three, two, one` | Sync a push to a flash countdown. |
| `Swing on my contact` | Trade timing call. |
| `I can smoke for plant` | Controller confirming plant support. |
| `Planting for [location]` | Spike orientation call. |
| `Play crossfire / Play time` | Post-plant role assignment. |
| `Save exit` | Get out alive; don't die for nothing. |
| `Wait for [number], then swing` | Retake coordination. |
| `He used [ability]` | Enemy ability burned — reduce threat level. |
| `No [ability]` | Enemy cannot use this ability right now. |
| `I have ult` | Self-report: ultimate is ready. |
| `Save / Drop me` | Economy management / weapon request. |
