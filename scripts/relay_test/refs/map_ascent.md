# Valorant — Ascent Map Reference

**Status:** Current as of June 2026  
**Pool status:** Active competitive map (returned S26 Act 3, replacing Bind)  
**Defining feature:** Massive open mid courtyard; **two mechanical destructible doors** (one per site); breakable wall panels in mid; shatterable garden window  
**Setting:** Venice, Italy — open-air canal city; cobblestone streets, market stalls, Italian architecture  
**Theme:** Warm stone, terracotta, vine-covered walls, market awnings, boats, timber-frame buildings  
**Release date:** June 2, 2020 (Valorant launch)

---

## Map Overview

Ascent is a classic two-site map with one of the largest mid sections in Valorant. The central courtyard (Mid) is the dominant strategic contest — the team that controls mid can split either site via Catwalk (→ A) or Market (→ B). Both sites have a unique **mechanical door** (500 HP, toggleable by a lever/switch nearby) that defenders can close to buy time; once destroyed, the door is permanently open for the rest of the round. Two **destructible wall panels** (400 HP each) in the mid connector area add further map-specific mechanics.

**Layout sketch (west → east):**
```
[Attacker Spawn]
      |
  A Lobby ─── A Main ─── A Site ─── Garden ─────────────────────┐
                                       |                          |
                                    [A Door]                 Defender Spawn (CT)
                                       |                          |

                                               |                  |
                                           Pizza ──── B Main ─── B Site

```

## Mechanical Features (Map-Unique)

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| A Door | Located at the A Main exit into site; 500 HP; toggled by A Switch on-site; destroys permanently when broken; health indicator: blue → yellow → red; ~13 Vandal shots to destroy; Raze satchels can destroy it in seconds |
| B Door | Located at the Market entrance to B site; 500 HP; same mechanics; switch is on a table INSIDE Workshop (away from the door itself) |
| Mid Wall Panels | Two destructible panels (400 HP each) in the Tiles / mid connector passageway; breaking them opens new sightlines from mid into flanking routes |
| Garden Window | Breakable glass window between A Garden and the Heaven/Rafters platform area; shatters on bullet contact or certain ability hits; opening it creates a new sightline between Garden and Heaven |
| Ultimate Orbs | One orb on the attacker side of A Main approach (pre-site); one orb in B Main (moved lower to ground level post Patch 4.04 for accessibility) |

## Strategic Notes for Relay Corpus

- **Mid is the map's engine** — Catwalk gives A access; Market gives B access; controlling mid court = map control. Whoever wins mid controls the tempo of the entire round.
- **A Main is the hardest lane to execute** — defenders hold Wine, A Peek, Heaven, and Hell simultaneously; requires significant utility investment to clear all angles safely.
- **Generator default plant** is the safest plant on A; post-plant from Tree/Garden cuts defender rotations while teammates hold from site.
- **Triple Box (B default)** is the safest plant on B; hold from Workshop/Boathouse and watch Stairs/Market entry.
- **Closing doors** is a double-edged mechanic: closing A door delays an A push but telegraphs defender intent and wastes A Switch time; closing B door early is lower-cost.
- **Tiles panels** — breaking them in mid opens sightlines that can catch defenders holding Market or Screens off-guard; often done with utility or Raze grenades.
- **Heaven control on A** gives information and angles on both A Main entry and Garden simultaneously; Heaven + Generator = near-impossible retake without utility.
- **Hell** is the hardest post-plant position to clear on A; a defender in Hell forces an attacker to throw utility before defusing.
- **Logs on B** is a mandatory clear before committing to site; a defender in Logs can deny the entire B Main push solo.
- **B Main** is narrow enough that a single Operator holds the entire corridor; Jett dash-peeking into B Main with an Op is the defining Jett play on Ascent.
- **Sova recon bolt through walls** into A Main, B Main, and mid courtyard is game-defining information every round.
- **Killjoy Lockdown** planted near the spike post-plant effectively stops most retakes on both sites.

## Lore / Flavor Notes

- Ascent was the **first map developed for Valorant** (per former Game Director Joe Zigler)
- Set in Venice, Italy; the floating/suspended city aesthetic is prominent
- Easter egg: a bench with **Tahm Kench's face** (League of Legends champion) visible on the map
- The open-air courtyard and canal aesthetic distinguish it visually from other Valorant maps
